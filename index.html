How it works

Reading reinvented for
the way students actually learn

Most students read at 80–120 words per minute. Their brain can process up to 400. That gap is wasted potential.

Sharp uses RSVP — Rapid Serial Visual Presentation — to flash one word at a time, eliminating the unconscious habit of re-reading. The result: students cover the same chapter in a third of the time, with better focus and retention.

It's not a gimmick. It's how your eyes were always meant to read — without the inefficiency of constant back-and-forth movement across a page.

Questions

Things parents usually ask

Is this just another screen addiction for my child?
Sharp is intentionally designed to be the opposite. There are no social features, no notifications, no infinite scroll, no rewards that keep pulling students back in. The app opens, students read their chapter, and they close it. That's it. The goal is to spend less time on the phone, not more.
Does speed reading actually help with retention?
RSVP technology specifically reduces subvocalization — the habit of mentally "saying" words as you read — which is the main bottleneck for most readers. Studies show that controlled speed reading, particularly for revision of already-familiar material, maintains comprehension while significantly increasing speed. Sharp is best used for revision, not first-time learning of complex concepts.
Which books are available? Are they the right ones for my child's exam?
Sharp includes all NCERT textbooks from Class 6 through 12, which are the primary source material for JEE and NEET. For UPSC, standard static GS texts are included. For CA, ICAI study material is pre-loaded. All books are organized by exam category so students see only what's relevant to them.
What if my child doesn't find it useful?
The 7-day free trial requires no credit card. Your child can use the full app for a week completely free. If it doesn't work for them, you simply don't subscribe. There's no charge, no cancellation needed.
